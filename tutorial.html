Tutorial

If you’ve ever seen someone in the ER talking to themselves while clicking a handheld mic, they’re probably using a Dragon dictaphone. It’s a voice recognition device that works with Dragon Medical One — software that transcribes your speech directly into the EMR. Basically, you talk, and it types.

In the emergency department, where time is tight and documentation is a beast, Dragon is a game changer. Instead of typing out a full HPI or physical exam while juggling five other patients, you can knock out your note just by dictating as you go. It’s especially helpful when you need to document something more nuanced — like a complicated MDM or a detailed procedure note — without having to scroll through templates or hunt for macros.

A lot of us use Dragon with auto-text: phrases or whole sections of notes we’ve pre-saved that trigger when we say a specific word or phrase. For example, I might say “insert normal ROS” and Dragon will drop in a full review of systems paragraph. It speeds things up a lot, especially during night shifts or when the department is slammed.

This site is a collection of commonly used Dragon auto-text phrases that you can browse, search, and copy into your own setup. Whether you're brand new to Dragon or just looking to expand your library, hopefully it saves you some time (and keystrokes).
